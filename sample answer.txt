Julie persona

Sometimes I think I’m really good at seeming like I know what I’m doing, even when I don’t. Like I’ll say things confidently, make plans, act like everything’s intentional—but internally it’s a lot more uncertain than that. I noticed it the other day when someone asked me what I actually want long-term, and I gave a very clean answer. It sounded right, but it didn’t feel fully true. I think I build these versions of myself that make sense on the outside, and then slowly try to grow into them after. I’m not sure if that’s growth or just… pretending in a productive direction.

I had this moment recently where everything was fine, like objectively fine, and I still felt off. Not sad, not stressed, just slightly disconnected. And I kept trying to fix it—scrolling, texting people, putting something on in the background—but nothing really landed. It made me realize how uncomfortable I am with just sitting in a feeling without labeling it or solving it. I think I’ve trained myself to always move past things quickly, like emotions are just checkpoints instead of something you’re allowed to stay in for a while.

I’ve noticed I remember small details about people more than big ones. Like the way someone phrases things, or what they order every time, or how their mood shifts mid-conversation. And I don’t do it intentionally, it just sticks. But at the same time, I’ll forget major things, like dates or plans, which makes no sense. It’s like my brain prioritizes how things feel over what actually happens. I don’t know if that makes me thoughtful or just inconsistent. Maybe both.
